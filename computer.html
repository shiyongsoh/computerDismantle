Defragmentation

DO NOT DEFRAGMENT A SOLID STATE DRIVE

Defragmentation is a way of reorganising data stored on a hard disk drive.

As you delete files from your hard drive, chunks of data will be remove and given a to another chunk of data to be written. Should it not have enough space on the hard drive, it will then write on somewhere else on the hard drive. If data retrival is needed, it will be much harder to track down the data stored on the hard drive, which will in turn delay the process.

What can you do?

Windows

Go into file explorer, and selected properties

Click onto the tools tab and press optimise

Click on optimise and have the computer doing the magic

Do not stop the fragmentation process or shut down the computer. As it will cause the disk to get fragmented even more
